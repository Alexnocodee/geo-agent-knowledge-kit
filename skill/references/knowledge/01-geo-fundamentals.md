# GEO Fundamentals

## Definition

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of improving how digital content and brand entities appear in AI-generated answers.

Traditional SEO optimizes for rankings and clicks. GEO optimizes for inclusion, citation, recommendation, and correct representation inside generated answers.

Related terms:

- AEO: Answer Engine Optimization
- AI SEO: SEO adapted to AI-mediated discovery
- LLMO: Large Language Model Optimization
- AI visibility: the measurable presence of a brand in AI answers

## What AI Systems Need

AI answer systems need content and sources that are:

- Easy to crawl
- Easy to parse
- Entity-clear
- Semantically aligned with the query
- Rich in extractable facts
- Supported by proof
- Repeated across trusted sources
- Fresh enough for time-sensitive topics

## GEO Is Not Magic

GEO does not mean manipulating a model directly. In practice, most work happens through:

- Better website architecture
- Better answer-style content
- Better entity signals
- Better external citations
- Better measurement
- Better alignment with buyer prompts

## Visibility Outcomes

Track several outcomes, not only "did the brand appear":

- Mention: the brand is named.
- Citation: the brand's URL is cited.
- Recommendation: the brand is suggested as an option.
- Absorption: facts, language, comparisons, or evidence from the page shape the answer.
- Sentiment/positioning: the brand is described accurately and favorably.
- Referral: traffic arrives from AI surfaces.
- Conversion: AI-referred sessions become pipeline or revenue.

## Practical GEO Levers

High-impact levers:

- Define the brand's category in plain language.
- Map buyer prompts by intent.
- Create pages for best-provider, alternatives, comparison, problem-solution, and niche/local prompts.
- Add structured FAQs and concise answer blocks.
- Use tables, definitions, steps, evidence, and comparison matrices.
- Add schema where appropriate.
- Build third-party mentions and citations.
- Measure repeatedly because AI answers vary.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating GEO as only metadata or `llms.txt`.
- Checking one ChatGPT answer once and calling it measurement.
- Publishing generic AI-written pages with no proof.
- Ignoring traditional SEO foundations.
- Building dashboards before fixing content and citations.
- Optimizing only in English for a multilingual market.
