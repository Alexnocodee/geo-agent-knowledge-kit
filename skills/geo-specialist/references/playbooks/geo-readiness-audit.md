# GEO Readiness Audit

Use this for a deeper audit than the mini-audit.

## Inputs

- Domain
- Market/language
- Product/service
- Competitors
- Business goal
- Current content assets
- Analytics/search access if available

## Workflow

1. Business and entity analysis
2. Technical SEO/GEO check
3. Prompt universe and intent map
4. Current page coverage
5. Competitor and SERP/source landscape
6. Proof and claims audit
7. External citation audit
8. Measurement readiness
9. Priority roadmap

## Deliverable

Produce:

- Executive summary
- One-sentence strategic diagnosis
- Readiness score
- Evidence notes
- Critical gaps by severity
- Competitor positioning and "how to win"
- Buyer prompt map
- Page/content backlog
- Technical checklist
- Citation opportunity backlog
- Measurement setup
- 30/60/90-day roadmap
- First next move

## Severity Levels

- Critical: blocks category/entity understanding or high-intent prompts.
- High: major gap in buyer journey, proof, or citation surface.
- Medium: useful improvement but not urgent.
- Low: polish or future optimization.

## Output Format

Use this structure for a full audit:

1. Data status
2. Executive diagnosis
3. Business/entity summary
4. AI Visibility Readiness Score
5. Critical gaps
6. Competitor positioning
7. Buyer prompt map
8. Page/content backlog
9. Citation opportunity backlog
10. Technical and schema notes
11. Measurement setup
12. 30/60/90-day roadmap
13. First next move

## Quality Bar

- Be specific enough that the client can assign tasks immediately.
- Tie every recommendation to a prompt group, citation gap, proof gap, or measurement gap.
- Distinguish owned-site work from external-citation work.
- Do not bury the strongest strategic idea near the end.
- If the user names a competitor, explain the competitor's likely winning angle and how the client should position against it.
