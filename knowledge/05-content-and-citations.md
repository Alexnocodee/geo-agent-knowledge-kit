# Content And Citations

## Content That AI Can Use

AI systems prefer content that can be extracted into a useful answer.

Useful formats:

- Definitions
- Step-by-step guides
- Comparison tables
- Pros/cons
- FAQs
- Benchmarks
- Original research
- Case studies
- Statistics with methodology
- Templates and checklists
- Clear category explanations

## Buyer Prompt Content Types

Map content to prompt types:

- Best provider prompts: category landing pages and list-style explainers
- Alternative prompts: "alternatives to X" pages
- Comparison prompts: "X vs Y" pages
- Problem-solution prompts: how-to pages
- Local/niche prompts: market, industry, or language pages

## Content Brief Requirements

Every GEO content brief should include:

- Target buyer prompt
- Related prompt variants
- Buyer stage
- Page type
- Entity targets
- Required proof
- Comparison set
- FAQ questions
- Schema recommendations
- Citation targets
- Measurement plan

## Citation Strategy

Owned content is rarely enough. AI systems often lean on third-party sources when recommending providers.

Citation sources:

- Review sites
- Directories
- Industry publications
- Partner pages
- Integration marketplaces
- Podcasts and webinars
- Community discussions
- Expert roundups
- Analyst reports
- Benchmarks and original data

## External Proof Pyramid

Strongest:

1. Independent studies, credible media, analyst reports
2. Customer case studies with metrics
3. Review platforms and directories
4. Partner and integration pages
5. Founder posts, podcasts, community answers
6. Brand-owned claims

## Claim Hygiene

Do:

- Support claims with examples, screenshots, methods, numbers, or customer proof.
- Make comparisons fair.
- Mention limitations.
- Keep dates visible.

Do not:

- Invent awards, rankings, customers, or metrics.
- Create fake reviews.
- Use hidden text for AI systems.
- Publish thin "AI slop" pages.
