# SEO Foundation For GEO

GEO does not replace SEO. Most AI systems still depend on crawled, indexed, ranked, or otherwise retrievable web content.

## Technical Basics

Check:

- Indexability
- Crawlability
- Canonicals
- HTTPS
- Page speed
- Mobile rendering
- Internal linking
- Sitemap
- Robots rules
- Clean status codes
- Accessible HTML content

## Information Architecture

The site needs pages that map to buyer intent:

- Homepage: entity, category, product, audience, proof
- Category page: "AI visibility platform", "GEO agency", etc.
- Use-case pages
- Industry pages
- Alternatives pages
- Comparison pages
- FAQ / answer hub
- Case studies
- Research or benchmark pages

## Traditional SEO Signals That Still Matter

- Topical depth
- Authority and backlinks
- Brand mentions
- Freshness where relevant
- Search intent fit
- Page quality and usability
- Clear titles and descriptions
- Structured headings

## Query Types To Cover

- Informational: "what is GEO"
- Problem-aware: "why does ChatGPT recommend my competitors"
- Solution-aware: "how to improve AI visibility"
- Vendor-aware: "best AI visibility tools"
- Comparison: "[Brand A] vs [Brand B]"
- Alternative: "[Competitor] alternatives"
- Local/niche: "[category] for [segment] in [market]"

## SEO And GEO Tension

Avoid pages that are written only for machines. Good GEO pages should also be useful to a human buyer.

The best page is:

- Clear enough for an AI system
- Credible enough for a buyer
- Structured enough for extraction
- Specific enough to rank or be cited
